# Spoorthi Fest: Comprehensive Context & Master Schedule
**Official Reference Document for Spoorthi AI Assistant**

## 1. Institutional Overview & Event Identity
* **About JNTUH:** JNTUH College of Engineering, Hyderabad, is a premier institution known for high educational standards, emphasizing both theoretical knowledge and practical exposure to produce highly skilled professionals.
* **About the ECE Department:** Established in **1973**, the Department of Electronics and Communication Engineering is a highly sought-after destination for engineering students in Telangana. It hosts academic programs for **1000+ students**, spanning undergraduate and multiple postgraduate specializations.
* **Event Definition:** **Spoorthi** (meaning "Inspiration" in Telugu) is the annual National Level Techno-Cultural Symposium organized by the JNTUH ECE Department. 
* **Legacy:** Established in **2004** as a technical fest, it evolved into a techno-cultural event in **2009**. It has been running successfully for **22 years**, drawing over 1,000 participants annually from colleges across Telangana and Andhra Pradesh (e.g., BVRIT, CBIT, ACE Engineering College).

## 4. Registration & Event Rules
* **Registration:** Available at the help desk near the ECE department entrance. Spot registration is available until **10:30 AM** for selected events.
* **Identification:** All participants must carry and wear their college ID cards inside the venue at all times.
* **Punctuality:** Participants must report **15 minutes** before the event start time. Late entries may be denied for workshops and presentations.
* **Team Limits:** Group events allow up to **4 members** per team. (Note: E-Papyrus is limited to 2 members).
* **Project Expo:** Teams must have their models completely set up by **2:00 PM**.
* **Judging:** Judges' decisions are final for all technical events.

---

## 5. Core Technical Competitions (The Symposium)
### Academic & Research
* **E-Papyrus (Paper Presentation):** The flagship event for presenting research on 5G/6G, Embedded Systems, Signal Processing, and IoT. Abstracts are limited to 200 words, final papers must be in IEEE format, and presentations in `.ppt`.
* **Poster Presentation:** Visual displays of innovative technical ideas.
* **Project Expo:** Showcasing working prototypes (e.g., Autonomous Drones, Smart City IoT, AI-driven assistive tech).

### Hardware & Software Challenges
* **Circuitrix / Circuit Innovation:** Designing and debugging complex electronic circuits using tools like Multisim or PSpice.
* **Matlab Mania:** Coding and simulation competition for mathematical and signal processing problems using MATLAB/Simulink.
* **Digi Logix:** Focused on digital electronics, boolean algebra, and logic gates.
* **Line Follower:** Autonomous robotics navigation challenge on a complex track.
* **Technical Quiz:** High-speed trivia covering the core ECE curriculum.

## 6. Emerging Tech & Industry Workshops (Pre-Fest Phase)
These sessions often feature industry collaboration (e.g., MathWorks, IEEE STB11759A, Tech Fluent Solutions).
* **AI & Programming:** "Magicball AI Festival" (Generative AI/Prompt Engineering), "Applied Python Programming."
* **Robotics & 3D Printing:** "3D - Modeling of Robotic ARM and overview of 3D printing."
* **VLSI & Chip Design:** "VLSI Design Methodology using FPGA" and "CMOS Layout design flow using Micro Wind Tool."
* **Telecom & Networks:** Seminars on "Wifi Evolution in IoT" and industrial Hackathons (Software-Defined Radio, Edge AI).

## 7. Cultural & Management Activities
* **Spot Events:** Informal, high-energy games including JAM (Just A Minute), Technical Treasure Hunts, and Photography contests.
* **Cultural Night:** Post-competition unwinding with music, dance, and "Jamming Truly" sessions.
* **Guest Lectures:** Keynotes from industry leaders (historically featuring companies like NVIDIA, Qualcomm, or ISRO).
* **PR Activities:** Flash mobs at prominent city locations (like Next Galleria Mall) to launch the fest.
