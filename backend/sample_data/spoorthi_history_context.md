# Spoorthi History and Legacy

This file is a clean, retrieval-friendly context source for answering broad questions about Spoorthi.

## Identity

- Spoorthi is the annual symposium organized by the Department of Electronics and Communication Engineering at JNTUH College of Engineering, Hyderabad.
- It is commonly described as a national-level technical or techno-cultural symposium.
- The name "Spoorthi" is associated with the idea of inspiration.

## Purpose

- Spoorthi provides a platform for technical competitions, workshops, project showcases, paper presentations, and cultural activities.
- It is positioned as the flagship symposium of the JNTUH ECE Department.
- It attracts participation from students across engineering colleges in Telangana and Andhra Pradesh.

## Origin and Growth

- Spoorthi is commonly traced back to the mid-2000s, with 2004 frequently cited as its starting point as a technical fest.
- Around 2009, it expanded from a purely technical fest into a techno-cultural symposium.
- Over time, it developed into a larger department flagship event with both academic and cultural programming.

## Academic Year Structure

- Phase 1: Pre-fest workshops and seminars, usually conducted from September to February.
- Phase 2: Main symposium days, usually held in March or April.

- 2004: Commonly cited as the beginning of Spoorthi as an ECE technical fest.

## Broad Summary

Spoorthi began as a technical fest and later evolved into a broader techno-cultural symposium. It remains closely associated with the identity of the JNTUH ECE Department and is presented as a long-running student-facing flagship event combining engineering, innovation, and cultural participation.
